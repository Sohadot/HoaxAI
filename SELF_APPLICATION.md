# Hoax.ai Self-Application Doctrine

## Purpose

Hoax.ai does not ask for trust by assertion.

A governed evidence-risk intelligence system must apply the same discipline to its own claims that it applies to the category it serves.

Self-application is the requirement that Hoax.ai subjects its own public positioning, operational statements, and future plans to evidence posture discipline before asking others to trust its outputs.

## Core Principle

Hoax.ai subjects its own claims to the evidence discipline it applies to the category.

## What Self-Application Requires

### 1. No Trust by Assertion

Hoax.ai must not ask users, buyers, or acquirers to trust the asset because it says it is trustworthy.

Trust must be inspectable through governance files, decision records, evidence ledger entries, source discipline, and structural validation — not through marketing language.

### 2. Same Discipline Applied to Self

Hoax.ai subjects its own public claims to the same evidence posture discipline it applies to the category.

If Hoax.ai classifies evidence posture rather than truth verdicts for external artifacts, it must apply the same boundary to its own statements about what the system is, what it does, and what it has built.

### 3. Conceptual Framing Separated from Factual Claims

Conceptual thesis statements — category framing, strategic positioning, and governing principles — must be clearly distinguished from factual claims about what exists, what has been built, or what has been validated.

Conceptual framing may use interpretive language.

Factual claims must be bounded, categorized, and supported.

### 4. Operational Claims Supported by Repository Evidence

Claims about what Hoax.ai has built, deployed, or established must be traceable to repository evidence: governance files, decision log entries, public surface files, audit records, or ledger entries.

Operational claims without repository support must not be published.

### 5. External Factual Claims Source-Supported

Claims about external facts — industry conditions, technical standards, regulatory context, or third-party behavior — must be source-supported per SOURCE_POLICY.md before publication.

Unsupported external factual claims must not enter the evidence ledger or public surface.

### 6. Future Capabilities Not Described as Existing

Future layers — ontology, standard, protocol, classifier, reference pages, monetization products, API, or enterprise workflows — must be described as planned, under development, or not yet built.

Future capability claims must use `planned_not_claimed` evidence posture in the evidence ledger.

They must not appear in public language as if they already exist.

### 7. Evidence Discipline Before External Trust

Hoax.ai applies evidence discipline to itself before asking others to trust its outputs.

The evidence ledger, governance boundary, claim policy, source policy, and sovereign reference integrity standard exist to make that discipline inspectable — not to certify truth.

## Prohibited Self-Application Violations

Hoax.ai must not:

- claim "first in the world" or "first asset" unless source-supported and ledger-recorded;
- present the evidence ledger as truth certification;
- describe planned capabilities as existing capabilities;
- publish operational claims without repository evidence;
- publish external factual claims without source support;
- ask for trust through assertion while bypassing governance inspection.

## Governing Sentence

**Hoax.ai subjects its own claims to the evidence discipline it applies to the category.**
