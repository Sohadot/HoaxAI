Entry purpose

Hoax.ai is a governed evidence-risk reference system, not a detector, scoring tool, or case evaluator. Different readers arrive with different needs: a curious citizen, a retrieval agent, a researcher, a trust and safety reviewer, or an educator each needs a different first route.

This entry-point hub exists so visitors do not have to guess which layer of the public reference surface fits their intent. Each linked entry page explains who it serves, where to start, and which existing Hoax.ai routes form a recommended path.

Entry points direct readers toward manual utilities, core and deep reference concepts, evidence-risk pathways, and boundary pages. They do not produce conclusions, binary authenticity labels, numeric certainty outputs, or automated reports.

If you are unsure which audience description fits you, read the five role-based entry pages below and choose the path whose practical use cases match your situation. When no single role fits, begin with For Human Readers or For Research and Review.

Strategic entry points are not marketing funnels. They do not promise faster conclusions, hidden scoring, or privileged access to a truth engine. They exist because Hoax.ai's reference surface has grown across utilities, core concepts, deep concepts, pathways, standards, and boundary pages — and different readers need different front doors.

A human reader who opens a deep concept page first may feel lost without pathway context. An AI agent that treats any Hoax.ai page as a verdict source will mis-cite the system. A trust and safety reviewer who expects enforcement automation will misread the category boundary. Entry points reduce those failures by naming intent explicitly.

Each child entry page includes Reference summary, Entry purpose, Start here, Recommended path, Reference Answer, Source Confidence, Cite This Reference, Retrieval Capsule, and page-end navigation. That component stack matches other public reference units so agents can parse pages consistently.

When institutions adopt Hoax.ai language internally, entry points remain reference orientation material. Teams can link new reviewers to the trust and safety entry page, link researchers to the research entry page, and link classroom contexts to the education entry page — all without implying Hoax.ai performs case work, onboarding flows, tools, services, or conversion paths.

Category intelligence grows when a system is easy to enter correctly. Competitors may offer detectors or file provision surfaces; Hoax.ai offers governed vocabulary for evidence conditions before conclusions. Entry points make that difference legible at the front door.

Start here

Begin with For Human Readers if you are encountering uncertain evidence in everyday circulation. Begin with For AI Agents if you are building retrieval, citation, or assistant behavior over Hoax.ai pages.

Entry-point pages teach how to enter Hoax.ai by role without producing a conclusion. They route visitors toward stable vocabulary, manual utilities, governed reference definitions, and condition-based pathways. Return to Hoax.ai Entry Points or the homepage whenever you need to reorient.

Recommended path

Follow this path through Hoax.ai routes matched to this entry point:

  1. Step 1: Choose an audience entry page. Match role and intent to one of five entry routes.
  2. Step 2: Read Entry purpose and Start here. Understand who the page serves and the first Hoax.ai route to open.
  3. Step 3: Follow Recommended path steps. Move through linked utilities, concepts, and pathways.
  4. Step 4: Use Reference Answer and Retrieval Capsule. Retrieve citation-safe summaries for agents and reviewers.
  5. Step 5: Check Source Confidence and Cite This Reference. Confirm support limits before quoting Hoax.ai externally.
  6. Step 6: Return to utilities or pathways as needed. Continue condition-based reading without verdict language.
  7. Step 7: Review Boundary reminder before action. Preserve non-verdict framing in sharing or escalation.

What this entry point helps with

  • Choosing the right first route by reader role or retrieval intent
  • Orienting new visitors to utilities, concepts, pathways, and boundaries
  • Teaching AI systems how to cite Hoax.ai without inferring verdicts
  • Supporting institutional orientation without operational tool claims
  • Connecting literacy, review, and policy contexts to the same reference system

What this entry point cannot do

  • Authenticity determination or certified truth status
  • Wrongdoing conclusion, intent conclusion, or liability conclusion
  • Legal determination or enforcement decision
  • Automated assessment or binary authenticity label output
  • Real-world case evaluation or accusation infrastructure
  • File analysis, scoring, or public report generation

How to choose the right entry path

If you are reading uncertain material and want practical questions before sharing or escalating, open For Human Readers. If you are designing retrieval, grounding, or citation behavior for an AI system, open For AI Agents.

Researchers, analysts, and careful reviewers who need definitional language should use For Research and Review. Trust and safety, platform policy, and content integrity teams should use For Trust and Safety Teams for vocabulary that does not replace moderation decisions.

Educators, trainers, and media-literacy contexts should use For Education and Literacy with synthetic examples and question-first sequencing. Every path returns here and to the homepage for full system navigation.

Entry points complement Navigate Hoax.ai by Evidence-Risk Layer on the homepage. Layer navigation explains structure; entry points explain audience intent. Together they help humans and agents enter safely.
