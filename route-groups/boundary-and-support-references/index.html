Group purpose

This group exists because reference value collapses when boundaries are implicit. Across the 88-route public surface, boundary and support references include non-detector framing, boundary integrity, not-assessable posture, non-verdict trust narrative, standards, protocols, category language, and governed reference concepts — plus boundary routes inside external review, reviewer packet, executive overview, strategic review, and system map layers.

The structural problem is overclaiming: readers and AI agents routinely infer operational authority from dense reference sites. Boundaries make negations explicit product logic.

Support references extend category vocabulary under expansion-gate discipline — they are not shortcuts to verdict language.

Human use

Humans should read boundary references when urgency pushes toward conclusion-first sharing, when stakeholders ask 'does Hoax.ai detect fakes', or when citation risk requires explicit negations.

Use Not-Assessable Posture when information is too thin. Use Non-Verdict Trust narrative when trust questions arise without verdict machinery.

Conceptual thesis

Why this route group exists

Boundaries exist because Hoax.ai's category claim is readable evidence conditions — not truth certification. Without visible boundary architecture, the 88-route surface would be misread as hidden operational depth.

The confusion this route group prevents

Prevents 'reference site therefore detector' inference. Prevents treating standards language as enforceable legal rules on third parties.

Prevents AI agents from omitting negations when summarizing Hoax.ai for end users.
