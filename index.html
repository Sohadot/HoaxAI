Synthetic Fragility

The old internet problem was misinformation: false or misleading claims moving through distribution systems.

The AI-era problem is deeper.

Artificial intelligence industrializes plausibility. A face can be generated. A voice can be cloned. A screenshot can be fabricated. A source can be imitated. A document can be forged. A quote can be invented. A context can be stripped, rebuilt, and redistributed.

This is synthetic fragility: the collapse of easy confidence in the evidence layer itself.

When evidence can be manufactured, trust cannot begin with appearance. Trust must begin with evidence structure.

Evidence Posture

Hoax.ai classifies evidence posture on evidence artifacts: the observable condition of an artifact or evidence chain before belief, action, publication, escalation, or institutional response.

Evidence posture is not a binary verdict. Source confidence, provenance integrity, contextual coherence, and synthetic risk signals each contribute to the posture of an artifact — not to any person, institution, or event connected to it.

A high-risk evidence posture does not mean that something is false. It means the evidence structure requires further examination before it can support confident action.

Four Initial Evidence Posture Pillars

Four initial pillars structure the planned Evidence Posture Classification Protocol.

01

Provenance Signals

Origin, custody, metadata, source chain, publication path, and traceability. The question is not only where content appeared — but whether its path can be meaningfully examined.

02

Forensic Coherence

Structural, linguistic, media, temporal, and format-based signals. Not absolute detection — coherence signals and uncertainty markers that indicate whether the evidence holds together.

03

Contextual Mapping

Whether the claim, media, source, or identity fits known temporal, geographic, institutional, and situational constraints. Context is part of evidence structure.

04

Evidence Posture Classification

A bounded assessment of confidence, uncertainty, risk signals, and recommended verification steps. Not a final truth verdict — a structured reading of evidence condition.

Governance Boundary

Hoax.ai is governed from the first public version.

  • It does not issue determinations about individuals, institutions, or events.
  • It does not claim to detect all synthetic media.
  • It does not produce a binary authenticity output.
  • It does not certify truth or guarantee detection.
  • It classifies evidence posture on artifacts only — not on connected subjects.

Hoax.ai classifies the evidence artifact — the image, recording, document, screenshot, source record, or media object — never the person, institution, brand, organization, or event connected to it. A statement about the condition of evidence is not a statement about people.

Classify the evidence artifact. Do not perform the verdict.
