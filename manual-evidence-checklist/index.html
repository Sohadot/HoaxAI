Before You Use This Checklist

Hoax.ai provides governed manual reference utilities. This page is a question guide only — not an upload surface, not a classifier, and not a verdict engine. Work through each group on paper or in discussion; nothing here is submitted for automated analysis.

Evidence-Risk Checklist

1. Source

  • Can the source be named without guesswork?
  • Is the source relationship clear (creator, publisher, distributor)?
  • Does the source have a trackable publication or custody path?
  • Is the source independent of the claim being promoted?

2. Provenance

  • Is origin information present and inspectable?
  • Has custody or chain-of-handling been documented?
  • Are metadata or format signals consistent with the stated origin?
  • Are there unexplained gaps in how the artifact arrived?
  • Would a reasonable reviewer know where to look next for origin evidence?

3. Context

  • Is temporal context stable (when was this captured or published)?
  • Is geographic or situational context intact?
  • Does surrounding narrative match what the artifact can support?
  • Has interpretive context been stripped or rebuilt in circulation?

4. Traceability

  • Can the claim be traced to a specific artifact or record?
  • Can the artifact be traced to a named source path?
  • Are intermediate copies or reposts documented?
  • Is claim-to-source linkage explicit rather than assumed?

5. Claim Drift

  • Has the public claim grown beyond what the artifact shows?
  • Has subject-level accusation replaced artifact-level description?
  • Are urgency or moral framing adding pressure to conclude early?
  • What part of the claim is artifact-supported versus narrative-added?

6. Boundary Integrity

  • Are you examining the artifact, not the person or institution connected to it?
  • Are conclusions limited to evidence structure rather than character judgment?
  • Have institutional or legal boundaries been respected?
  • Is there a clear line between posture language and verdict language?

7. Evidence Posture

  • Given the answers above, what posture vocabulary fits (uncertainty, limitation, gap)?
  • Are there enough conditions for confident action, or only partial structure?
  • What would need to change before a stronger posture could be stated?
  • Can you describe condition without collapsing into true/false shorthand?

How to Use This Checklist

Read each group in order. Answer honestly where information is missing — absence of evidence is itself a structural signal. Use the checklist in meetings, editorial review, or personal decision-making before sharing material. Pair it with the Evidence Posture Map and Evidence-Risk Questions for fuller framing.

What This Checklist Cannot Do

This checklist cannot certify truth status, certify authenticity, assign numeric certainty outputs, detect synthetic media automatically, or replace forensic, legal, or institutional review. It does not accept uploads and does not produce automated reports.

When to Stop and Avoid a Verdict

Stop when provenance is broken, context has collapsed, traceability is absent, or claim drift has moved from artifact to accusation. In those conditions, the disciplined response is bounded uncertainty — not a public true/false label.
