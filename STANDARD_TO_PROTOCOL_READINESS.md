## Purpose

This document prepares a future **Evidence Posture Protocol v1** sprint from the existing public standard at `/standard/evidence-posture/`. It is not a public page, not a protocol route, and not operational governance expansion. **No public protocol route is created in Sprint 60.**

---

## What the Standard May Later Become in Protocol

Evidence Posture Standard v1 already defines elements that a future protocol may operationalize as **ordered evaluation steps**, not verdict shortcuts:

| Standard element | Potential protocol step |
|------------------|-------------------------|
| Evidence support conditions | Prerequisite checks before posture assignment |
| Posture states (Supported, Qualified, Limited, Not Assessable, Out of Scope) | Governed assignment outcomes |
| EPS-001 through EPS-014 | Mandatory principle gates in assignment sequence |
| Allowed output language | Permitted statement templates |
| Prohibited output language | Hard refusal rules |
| Standard matrix | Condition-to-posture mapping |
| Boundary rules | Conversion prohibitions enforced before publication |

A future protocol may **evaluate** artifact availability, source confidence, provenance continuity, context completeness, claim-source traceability, chain continuity, claim stability, interpretation risk, attribution boundary risk, and output boundary risk—then assign a posture state and emit bounded language.

---

## What Must Remain Prohibited Until a Protocol Sprint

Until a dedicated protocol sprint authorizes operational layers under separate gates:

- No public protocol route (e.g. `/protocol/`) may be created.
- No public engine, classifier, upload workflow, scoring system, API, analytics, forms, or public tool behavior is authorized.
- No automated fake/real verdict, subject guilt assignment, or deception certification.
- No DNS/Cloudflare, custom domain launch, or monetization tied to protocol behavior.
- No protocol that invents authority not present in the reference layer and standard.

Sprint 60 ends at **readiness**. Protocol creation is Sprint 61+ under governance.

---

## What a Future Protocol May Evaluate

A governed protocol may later:

- Walk support conditions in standard order.
- Apply EPS principle gates before posture assignment.
- Classify statements as supported, qualified, limited, unsupported, or not assessable.
- Refuse prohibited output language automatically in governed internal systems.
- Document which reference concepts activated each limitation.
- Produce audit-ready posture records for institutional review.

Evaluation means **structured assignment under the standard**—not public accusation, moderation action, or truth adjudication.

---

## What a Future Protocol Must Not Conclude

A protocol must not:

- Issue fake/real verdicts or truth certification.
- Assign guilt, intent, authorship, deception, misconduct, or responsibility to subjects.
- Treat provenance gaps as manipulation proof.
- Treat claim drift as deception by default.
- Treat evidence limitation as falsehood proof.
- Treat interpretation risk as blame or falsity proof.
- Treat evidence chain continuity as truth certification.
- Replace human institutional review where legal or moderation decisions are required.

The protocol obeys the standard. It does not override Artifact–Subject Separation or Output Boundary discipline.

---

## Why Protocol Must Follow the Standard

Hoax.ai authority emerges from the reference layer and Evidence Posture Standard v1—not from abstract governance loops or detector-style product framing. A protocol that invents its own vocabulary would:

- Break cross-link integrity across sixteen reference pages.
- Reintroduce verdict language the standard prohibits.
- Undermine buyer and institutional trust in Hoax.ai as a standard-bearing asset.

Protocol work must **cite EPS principles**, **use standard posture states**, and **respect allowed/prohibited language** defined at `/standard/evidence-posture/`.
