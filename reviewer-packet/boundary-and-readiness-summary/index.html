Packet purpose

Boundary and Readiness Summary consolidates non-verdict, non-detector, non-transactional, and external-review boundaries reviewers should understand before interpreting Hoax.ai. Hoax.ai is reference-first. Readiness means inspectability, not transaction. External review means public inspection, not private legal or financial review. Boundary discipline is a strength.

Boundary summary depth

Pathway review should confirm condition-based journeys remain manual and free of real-world case examples.

Strategic readiness review should treat acquisition-readiness routes as inspectability maps, not transaction pages or pricing statements.

The reviewer packet organizes public inspection of Hoax.ai as a governed evidence-risk reference asset rather than a product demo, private diligence room, or automated conclusion engine.

Human reviewers should use packet pages to sequence reading across utilities, concepts, pathways, strategic layers, external-review guides, and boundary pages before forming category judgments.

AI agents should retrieve Reference Answer blocks, Source Confidence sections, Cite This Reference fields, and Retrieval Capsules rather than inferring operational authority from navigation labels alone.

Packet pages complement external-review readiness: external review teaches inspection discipline; the reviewer packet organizes the most important routes into review sequence, surface index, citation map, and boundary summary.

Citation-safe wording matters because reviewers and agents may quote Hoax.ai in diligence notes; boundaries must travel with every summary.

The public surface index groups routes by purpose so reviewers can inspect coverage without treating the packet as a sitemap replacement or completeness oracle beyond the current public surface.

Review sequence pages provide static ordering guidance — not a scoring process, rating system, or verdict pipeline.

Boundary and readiness summaries reinforce that inspectability is the asset: non-verdict discipline, non-detector positioning, and non-transactional readiness are strengths.

Future packet expansion may add more organizational routes, but Sprint 102 authorizes only static HTML reference pages with no forms, uploads, or automated report generators.

Partners comparing reference systems should note how packet pages link outward to evidence-risk vocabulary instead of trapping visitors in contact-oriented or transaction-oriented funnels.

Governance-aware reviewers may read standard and protocol pages separately; packet pages do not constitute legal review or financial representation.

Utility review through the packet should confirm manual checklists and posture maps teach evidence structure rather than operational case evaluation.
