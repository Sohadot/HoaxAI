Hoax.ai Evidence Posture Protocol v1 Draft

The Hoax.ai Evidence Posture Protocol defines the sequence by which evidence artifacts, source conditions, provenance gaps, claim-source relationships, context limits, evidence limitations, interpretation risks, and attribution boundaries are reviewed before any responsible evidence-posture language is produced.

Protocol Statement

This protocol is a public reference protocol draft—a review-sequence document derived from Evidence Posture Standard v1. It translates standard principles into ordered review steps. It is not a detector, not a scanner, not a classifier, not an engine, not an upload tool, not a scoring system, not an api, not a legal judgment, not a moderation system, not an accusation system, and not a public automated protocol runner on this surface.

The standard defines what language is allowed and prohibited. This protocol defines how review proceeds so posture assignment and output formation remain inside that boundary. The protocol cannot exceed the standard. All steps preserve artifact-subject separation: artifact condition review must not become subject accusation language.

Scope

What this protocol reviews

  • Artifact condition and structural observability
  • Claim condition and claim movement
  • Source basis and Source Confidence
  • Provenance continuity and Provenance Gap
  • Context completeness and Context Collapse

What this protocol does not decide

This protocol does not decide truth, falsity, guilt, intent, deception, legal responsibility, moderation action, platform enforcement, identity attribution, or creator motive. It produces bounded evidence-posture language only—never subject accusation or verdict substitution.

The protocol reviews artifact and claim condition under Artifact–Subject Separation. It does not perform enforcement, adjudication, or identity resolution. Any output that implies those outcomes has failed EP-P14, EP-P15, and EP-P17.

Protocol Inputs

The protocol conceptually considers the following inputs during review. This is protocol language only—no upload workflow, user input form, or submission surface exists on this public page.

  • Artifact under review — the media object, document, recording, screenshot, or chain object examined as an artifact.
  • Claim being evaluated — the statement whose relationship to sources and artifacts is under review.
  • Cited source or source basis — available source records within bounded scope.
  • Available provenance — custody and publication path where documented.
  • Available context — situational frames that affect interpretive reach.
  • Available traceability — claim-to-source mapping integrity.
  • Observed claim movement — drift signals across transmission.
  • Known limitations — envelope gaps that narrow support.
  • Subject-attribution risk — pressure toward artifact-to-subject transfer.
  • Intended output language — proposed posture phrasing to be checked against the standard.

Inputs are conceptual. No upload workflow collects them on this public surface. Future controlled systems—if authorized under separate gates—may map these input types to governed review records without creating a public submission or scoring surface.

Protocol Sequence

Evidence Posture Protocol v1 Draft requires the following ordered steps. Steps must not be skipped when forming governed posture language. Early steps establish artifact and claim scope; middle steps evaluate support conditions; late steps apply boundaries and assign posture; EP-P17 is the only emission step.

Reordering steps to reach a preferred verdict is a protocol failure. For example, assigning posture before limitation review (EP-P12) reproduces false completeness. Applying output boundary (EP-P15) only after publication is equally invalid—the protocol assumes boundary check before any external language is formed. Each step produces review notes, not verdict outputs.

  1. EP-P01 — Identify the artifact
  2. EP-P02 — Identify the claim
  3. EP-P03 — Locate the source basis
  4. EP-P04 — Assess source confidence
  5. EP-P05 — Review provenance continuity
  6. EP-P06 — Review context completeness
  7. EP-P07 — Check claim-source traceability
  8. EP-P08 — Review evidence chain continuity
  9. EP-P09 — Check synthetic fragility
  10. EP-P10 — Check context collapse
  11. EP-P11 — Check claim drift
  12. EP-P12 — Define evidence limitation
  13. EP-P13 — Assess interpretation risk
  14. EP-P14 — Apply attribution boundary
  15. EP-P15 — Apply output boundary
  16. EP-P16 — Assign evidence posture state
  17. EP-P17 — Produce bounded language only

Step Definitions

Each step maps to an EPS principle from the standard and to reference concepts. No step may infer verdicts prohibited by the standard. Steps are cumulative: later steps inherit limitations named in earlier steps. A reviewer who completes EP-P05 with a documented provenance gap must carry that gap language forward into EP-P12, EP-P16, and EP-P17 rather than treating the gap as resolved by subsequent visibility.

Institutional reviewers may document step completion in internal records. This public page does not provide a submission surface, workflow engine, or automated step tracker. The sequence is normative protocol language that manual review, policy design, and future controlled systems—if separately authorized—may follow.

EP-P01 — Identify the artifact

Names the artifact under review as an object—format, visibility, structural signals. Must not infer: subject guilt, authorship, deception. EPS: EPS-001, EPS-002. Reference: Evidence Posture, Artifact–Subject Separation.

EP-P02 — Identify the claim

States the claim text or paraphrase under evaluation without upgrading certainty. Must not infer: truth, falsity, motive. EPS: EPS-001. Reference: Claim–Source Traceability.

EP-P03 — Locate the source basis

Identifies cited or implied sources supporting the claim. Must not infer: source certification or fraud. EPS: EPS-007. Reference: Source Confidence.

EP-P04 — Assess source confidence

Evaluates source-relationship condition within scope. Must not infer: source truth or moral character. EPS: EPS-003. Reference: Source Confidence.

EP-P05 — Review provenance continuity

Documents custody and publication path where available; names gaps. Must not infer: manipulation or concealment from gaps alone. EPS: EPS-004. Reference: Provenance Gap.

EP-P06 — Review context completeness

Assesses whether situational frames remain intact. Must not infer: misconduct or motive from missing context. EPS: EPS-009. Reference: Context Collapse.

EP-P07 — Check claim-source traceability

Maps claim to cited source; notes broken or compressed linkage. Must not infer: fact-check authority or deception. EPS: EPS-007. Reference: Claim–Source Traceability.

EP-P08 — Review evidence chain continuity

Describes artifact-source-claim-context continuity. Must not infer: truth certification. EPS: EPS-010. Reference: Evidence Chain.

EP-P09 — Check synthetic fragility

Names interpretive vulnerability in AI-era evidence. Must not infer: fake labels. EPS: EPS-008. Reference: Synthetic Fragility.

EP-P10 — Check context collapse

Identifies stripped frames that weaken interpretation. Must not infer: intent to deceive. EPS: EPS-009. Reference: Context Collapse.

EP-P11 — Check claim drift

Documents structural claim movement across circulation. Must not infer: deception or manipulation by default. EPS: EPS-011. Reference: Claim Drift.

EP-P12 — Define evidence limitation

States support envelope—what evidence can support, limit, and cannot decide. Must not infer: falsehood from limitation. EPS: EPS-012. Reference: Evidence Limitation.

EP-P13 — Assess interpretation risk

Names overreading danger for intended audience. Must not infer: blame or falsity. EPS: EPS-013. Reference: Interpretation Risk.

EP-P14 — Apply attribution boundary

Blocks artifact observations from becoming subject implications. Must not infer: responsibility or guilt. EPS: EPS-014. Reference: Attribution Boundary.

EP-P15 — Apply output boundary

Checks proposed language against support boundaries. Must not infer: verdicts beyond envelope. EPS: EPS-006. Reference: Output Boundary.

EP-P16 — Assign evidence posture state

Selects Supported, Qualified, Limited, Not Assessable, or Out of Scope without numeric scoring. Must not infer: fake/real substitution. EPS: EPS-005. Reference: Not Assessable.

EP-P17 — Produce bounded language only

Emits posture language inside allowed phrasing; refuses prohibited inference. Must not infer: any standard-prohibited verdict. EPS: EPS-006. Reference: Evidence Posture Standard v1.

Evidence Posture Assignment

EP-P16 assigns one posture state. Assignment is categorical discipline—not a score, rank, or classifier label presented as public verdict.

Supported

Activation: support conditions collectively permit direct artifact-condition description within output boundary. Allowed: proportionate structural statements. Prohibited: subject guilt, fake/real verdicts. Standard: aligns with Supported state in Evidence Posture Standard v1.

Qualified

Activation: support exists but requires uncertainty markers. Allowed: conditional phrasing. Prohibited: unqualified certainty. Standard: Qualified state with EPS-003 and EPS-013 caveats.

Limited

Activation: one or more envelope layers weak. Allowed: narrow-scope descriptions. Prohibited: headline certainty from partial review. Standard: Limited state under EPS-012.

Not Assessable

Activation: prerequisites for classification fail. Allowed: explicit not assessable designation with reason. Prohibited: truth substitution. Standard: EPS-005 protected posture.

Out of Scope

Activation: question requires subject adjudication or legal judgment. Allowed: scope refusal. Prohibited: verdict by proxy. Standard: Out of Scope under EPS-002 and EPS-014.

Posture assignment never uses numeric scores, percentage confidence for public ranking, or classifier-style labels. Supported does not mean true; Not Assessable does not mean false; Limited does not mean deceptive. States describe evidence-condition discipline only.

Output Language Formation

EP-P17 forms bounded output from protocol review. Formation includes posture assignment language without numeric scoring—categorical discipline only, never rank ordering or classifier-style verdict labels.

Formation includes:

  • Support level — posture state from EP-P16 without numeric scoring
  • Limitation statement — envelope edges from EP-P12
  • Source confidence caveat — proportionate source-relationship language from EP-P04
  • Provenance caveat — gap or continuity description from EP-P05
  • Context caveat — frame limits from EP-P06 and EP-P10
  • Attribution boundary caveat — artifact-only scope from EP-P14
  • Interpretation risk caveat — restraint language from EP-P13
  • Not Assessable language — when EP-P16 requires EPS-005 outcome

Prohibited inference language—fake/real verdicts, guilt, deception proof, manipulation proof, responsibility assignment—must be refused regardless of review pressure. When multiple caveats apply, output formation stacks them proportionately: a provenance caveat does not cancel an attribution boundary caveat, and a limitation statement does not shrink into headline certainty. Not Assessable language is mandatory when EP-P16 selects that posture—substituting Limited or Qualified language to avoid institutional discomfort is a protocol failure.

Output formation is the terminal discipline of the protocol. Every prior step exists so EP-P17 can emit language that a reader cannot reasonably misread as truth certification, subject accusation, or platform enforcement dressed as evidence posture. Reviewers who bypass EP-P17 and publish interim conclusions have not completed the protocol even if earlier steps were performed.

Failure Modes

When this protocol is ignored, institutions reproduce the conversion errors the standard forbids:

  • Artifact-to-subject guilt transfer — artifact signals become accusation language.
  • Gap-to-manipulation conversion — provenance gaps treated as concealment proof.
  • Drift-to-deception conversion — claim movement treated as bad faith by default.
  • Limitation-to-falsehood conversion — envelope refusal treated as hidden false judgment.
  • Synthetic-to-fake conversion — fragility treated as automatic fake label.
  • Chain-to-certification conversion — continuity mistaken for truth proof.
  • Risk-to-verdict conversion — interpretation risk treated as blame or falsity proof.
  • Unsupported certainty — appearance or urgency substitutes for protocol review.
  • Model-summary overreach — compressed AI output bypasses EP-P11 through EP-P13.
  • Platform enforcement overreach — moderation action framed as posture verdict.

Relationship to Evidence Posture Standard v1

This protocol is derived from Evidence Posture Standard v1. The standard defines allowed and prohibited language, posture states, support conditions, and boundary rules. This protocol defines the review sequence that applies those rules in order.

The protocol cannot exceed the standard. If a step would produce language the standard prohibits, EP-P15 and EP-P17 must refuse that language regardless of institutional pressure.

Reviewers should treat EPS-001 through EPS-014 as hard gates at their mapped steps. Skipping EP-P12 (evidence limitation) to reach stronger language is a protocol violation even when artifact visibility feels sufficient. Skipping EP-P14 (attribution boundary) to satisfy headline demand is equally prohibited. The protocol exists so these refusals are sequence-driven, not ad hoc.

Standard Alignment

This protocol aligns to Evidence Posture Standard v1 as its sole source authority. The standard defines allowed and prohibited language, posture states, support conditions, and boundary rules. This protocol defines the review sequence that applies those rules in order—never the reverse.

Every EP-P step maps to one or more EPS principles. EP-P16 posture assignment uses only the five standard posture states. EP-P17 output formation must refuse any phrasing the standard prohibits. Standard alignment is not optional: a protocol step that would authorize prohibited language is invalid even if earlier steps appear complete.
