## Draft Thesis

Artifact–subject separation is a core Hoax.ai governance boundary: examination applies to evidence artifacts and chains, not to persons, institutions, or events as accusation targets. The boundary exists to keep reference language disciplined, to prevent posture vocabulary from collapsing into blame narratives, and to ensure future reference pages remain category infrastructure rather than accusation infrastructure. This draft defines that boundary for internal reference use without authorizing public surfaces, operational systems, or external factual adjudication.

## Definition and Scope

An artifact, in this context, is material treated as evidence under governance: documents, media objects, chains of custody references, and related metadata when discussed as evidentiary objects rather than as identity proxies. A subject, in the prohibited sense, is a person, institution, or event positioned as the target of guilt, fraud determination, impersonation verdict, or institutional blame through Hoax.ai reference language. Review refinement clarified that separation applies to reference prose and planning language, not to external adjudication of any party.

Separation requires that reference units describe what can be said about artifacts under posture, provenance, and traceability rules without converting artifact discussion into subject accusation. The scope includes boundary definitions, allowed posture framing, prohibited accusation framing, and interface embodiment constraints that prevent UI language from reintroducing subject targeting.

The scope excludes fraud determination services, guilt indexes, institutional blame archives, and any reference pattern that treats Hoax.ai as an accusation platform. It excludes numeric subject risk scores, ranking tables, and narrative frames that substitute artifact examination with moral or legal verdict language about identifiable parties.

## Governance Boundary

This draft must not be read as authorization to publish accusation-oriented pages, fraud verdict resources, or institutional blame indices. It must not be read as a policy that Hoax.ai adjudicates external disputes. It must not be read as enabling interface patterns that center subjects rather than artifacts.

Repository visibility does not create site publicity. This markdown file is governance working material stored under `_internal_drafts/reference/`. It is not registered as a route, not sitemap eligible, not linked from public navigation, and not published with public metadata. Deployment remains blocked.
