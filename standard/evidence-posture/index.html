Hoax.ai Evidence Posture Standard v1

The Hoax.ai Evidence Posture Standard defines how evidence artifacts, source conditions, provenance gaps, claim-source relationships, context limits, attribution boundaries, and interpretation risks may be described without converting evidence condition into truth verdict, subject accusation, or unsupported certainty.

Standard Statement

This standard governs responsible language for describing the condition of evidence under AI-era synthetic fragility. It is derived from the Hoax.ai public reference layer and converts reference concepts into normative output discipline. Hoax.ai Evidence Posture Standard v1 is a public reference standard—a language and output discipline for evidence-condition description—not a fake/real detector, not a truth engine, not a lie detector, not a moderation system, not a scoring system, not an upload workflow, not an API, not a public classifier, not a legal judgment system, and not an accusation system.

The standard exists so that platforms, analysts, researchers, journalists, trust and safety teams, compliance reviewers, and AI systems can share a governed vocabulary for what may be said about artifact condition, what must be refused, and when evidence is limited or not assessable. It constrains future reports and engines without authorizing any operational public tool on this surface.

Scope

What this standard covers

Hoax.ai Evidence Posture Standard v1 applies to governed description of evidence artifacts, claims, sources, provenance, context, traceability, output language, interpretation limits, and attribution boundaries. It defines posture states, support conditions, allowed phrasing, prohibited phrasing, and boundary rules that future protocol and engine layers must obey if authorized under separate gates.

  • Evidence artifacts — documents, media objects, screenshots, recordings, metadata bundles, and chain objects examined as artifacts.
  • Claims — statements whose relationship to sources and artifacts may be traced, limited, or refused.
  • Sources — source records and source-relationship conditions within bounded scope.
  • Provenance — custody, origin path, and chain continuity where documented.
  • Context — situational frames that affect interpretive reach.
  • Traceability — claim-to-source mapping integrity across circulation.
  • Output language — permissible and impermissible phrasing for posture description.
  • Interpretation limits — restraint when overreading risk is high.
  • Attribution boundaries — separation between artifact observation and subject implication.

What this standard does not cover

This standard does not issue truth verdicts, assign guilt, certify deception intent, determine legal responsibility, make content moderation decisions, operate automated detection, produce scores, or function as a public classification tool. It does not authorize upload intake, API endpoints, analytics dashboards, or user-submitted scoring on the public surface. It is normative reference authority, not operational product behavior.

Governing Principles

The following principles form the constitutional core of Evidence Posture Standard v1. Each principle is numbered for protocol and audit traceability.

  • EPS-001 — Evidence condition before conclusion. Posture description precedes any institutional action. Conclusions about subjects, events, or truth must not be substituted for artifact-condition review.
  • EPS-002 — Artifact condition is not subject guilt. Artifact–Subject Separation is mandatory. Artifact-level signals must not transfer into subject accusation.
  • EPS-003 — Source confidence is not source certification. Source Confidence describes relationship condition within bounds; it does not certify that a source is truthful or fraudulent.
  • EPS-004 — Provenance gaps limit interpretation but do not prove manipulation. A Provenance Gap narrows what may be said; it is not proof of concealment or deceptive intent.
  • EPS-005 — Not Assessable is a valid protected posture. Not Assessable is a responsible outcome when prerequisites fail—not failure, embarrassment, or hidden judgment.
  • EPS-006 — Output must remain inside support boundaries. The Output Boundary contract governs all governed statements about artifact condition.
  • EPS-007 — Claim-source traceability governs claim strength. Claim–Source Traceability determines how strongly a claim may be described relative to its cited basis.
  • EPS-008 — Synthetic fragility is not automatic falsehood. Synthetic Fragility names interpretive vulnerability in AI-era evidence; it does not mean an artifact is fake.
  • EPS-009 — Context collapse does not prove motive. Context Collapse weakens interpretive reach; it does not prove misconduct or intent.
  • EPS-010 — Evidence chain continuity is not truth certification. An Evidence Chain describes continuity; chain presence does not certify truth.
  • EPS-011 — Claim drift does not prove deception. Claim Drift names structural claim movement; drift is not deception by default.
  • EPS-012 — Evidence limitation defines responsible speech. Evidence Limitation is credibility infrastructure, not weakness or verdict substitution.
  • EPS-013 — Interpretation risk requires output restraint. High Interpretation Risk triggers narrower language; interpretation risk is not accusation.
  • EPS-014 — Attribution boundary prevents subject-transfer overclaim. The Attribution Boundary blocks artifact-to-subject guilt transfer without independent support.

Evidence Posture States

Evidence Posture Standard v1 defines five posture states. These are language disciplines, not numeric scores. No public scoring workflow, classifier surface, or detector surface is authorized by this standard page.

Supported

Activates when: artifact availability, source confidence, provenance continuity, context completeness, and claim-source traceability collectively support a statement about artifact or chain condition within output boundary discipline.

Allowed language: Direct artifact-condition description proportionate to available layers; structural observations with clear scope.

Prohibited language: Subject guilt, deception certification, fake/real verdicts, or responsibility assignment beyond artifact scope.

Influencing concepts: Evidence Posture, Evidence Limitation, Output Boundary.

Qualified

Activates when: support exists but uncertainty markers, scope limits, or conditional dependencies must accompany the statement to remain proportionate.

Allowed language: Conditional phrasing, explicit uncertainty, narrow scope declarations.

Prohibited language: Unqualified certainty; language that collapses qualification into verdict.

Influencing concepts: Source Confidence, Provenance Gap, Interpretation Risk.

Limited

Activates when: one or more envelope layers are weak while others remain partially available; only narrow statements are supportable.

Allowed language: Limited-scope artifact description; explicit naming of which layers are unavailable.

Prohibited language: Statements that treat limited visibility as full support; headline certainty from excerpt-only review.

Influencing concepts: Evidence Limitation, Context Collapse, Claim Drift.

Not Assessable

Activates when: prerequisites for responsible classification fail—missing artifact access, broken traceability, insufficient chain context, or envelope collapse that forbids further posture assignment.

Allowed language: Explicit not assessable designation with reason; refusal of unsupported statements.

Prohibited language: Fake/real substitution; treating not assessable as proof of falsehood or deception.

Influencing concepts: Not Assessable, Evidence Chain, Claim–Source Traceability.

Out of Scope

Activates when: the question posed requires subject adjudication, legal judgment, moderation action, or truth verdict outside evidence-posture language.

Allowed language: Scope refusal; redirection to appropriate institutional process without implying artifact verdict.

Prohibited language: Performing the verdict by proxy; subject accusation dressed as artifact review.

Influencing concepts: Artifact–Subject Separation, Attribution Boundary, Output Boundary.

Evidence Support Conditions

Posture assignment under this standard depends on evaluating support conditions—not on appearance, urgency, or narrative pressure. The following conditions affect which posture state and output language are permissible.

  • Artifact availability — whether the artifact itself can be examined with sufficient fidelity for bounded description.
  • Source confidence — structural support for treating a source record as reliable within declared scope, without certifying truth.
  • Provenance continuity — whether custody and publication path can be meaningfully described or are gapped.
  • Context completeness — whether situational frames remain intact or have collapsed through excerpting, reframing, or redistribution.
  • Claim-source traceability — whether claims map to cited sources without drift, compression, or broken linkage.
  • Evidence chain continuity — whether artifact, source, claim, and context remain connectable across review—not whether the chain certifies truth.
  • Claim stability — whether the claim under review matches its source basis or has drifted across transmission.
  • Interpretation risk — whether readers, platforms, or models are likely to overread available signals.
  • Attribution boundary risk — whether artifact observations are being pressured into subject-level implication.
  • Output boundary risk — whether proposed language exceeds what support conditions can carry.

Support conditions are evaluated together. A single strong layer does not override weakness in others. Evidence limitation language must name which layers support, which layers limit, and which layers cannot decide subject guilt, intent, or deception.

Allowed Output Language

When governed by this standard, Hoax.ai may use language that describes evidence condition within support boundaries. Examples include:

  • “The available evidence supports a limited posture.”
  • “The source basis is not sufficient for a stronger conclusion.”
  • “The artifact can be described, but subject attribution is not supported.”
  • “The claim appears to have drifted from the cited source.”
  • “The evidence condition is not assessable from the available material.”
  • “The provenance gap limits interpretation.”
  • “The interpretation risk is high.”
  • “Artifact-level condition can be described; responsibility language is out of scope.”
  • “Qualified posture is appropriate given source ambiguity and intact traceability.”
  • “Output must remain inside the support envelope defined by evidence limitation.”

Allowed language preserves artifact-subject separation, names limitations as credibility infrastructure, and treats not assessable outcomes as valid protected postures rather than failures.

Prohibited Output Language

Unless independently supported outside the evidence posture system, Hoax.ai must not use the following classes of language in governed output:

  • “This is fake.”
  • “This is real.”
  • “This person lied.”
  • “This source is fraudulent.”
  • “This proves deception.”
  • “This proves manipulation.”
  • “The subject is responsible.”
  • “The creator intended to deceive.”
  • “The claim is false” when only evidence limitation exists.
  • Any verdict language unsupported by the evidence condition.

Prohibited language includes scanner-style certainty, detector-style verdict phrasing, classifier-style labels presented as public verdicts, and score-based rankings that substitute for posture discipline. This standard forbids converting limitation into falsehood, drift into deception, gaps into manipulation, chains into certification, or risk into blame.

Boundary Rules

Evidence Posture Standard v1 enforces seven strict conversion prohibitions. These rules protect the reference layer from collapse into verdict infrastructure:

  • No artifact-to-subject guilt transfer. Artifact condition must not become subject accusation without independent support outside posture language.
  • No limitation-to-falsehood conversion. Evidence limitation is not proof that the underlying claim is false.
  • No drift-to-deception conversion. Claim movement does not prove deception, manipulation, or motive by default.
  • No gap-to-manipulation conversion. Provenance gaps limit interpretation; they do not prove concealment or manipulation.
  • No chain-to-certification conversion. Evidence chain continuity is not truth certification.
  • No risk-to-verdict conversion. Interpretation risk requires restraint; it does not prove falsehood or blame.
  • No synthetic-to-fake conversion. Synthetic fragility names vulnerability; it does not issue fake labels.

These boundary rules apply to human readers, analysts, platforms, AI systems, journalists, compliance workflows, and model-generated summaries alike. Classify the evidence artifact. Do not perform the verdict.

Institutional Usefulness

Evidence Posture Standard v1 matters because AI-era evidence environments punish institutions that speak with false completeness. Platforms need shared language for escalation holds without implying a public detector. Journalists need posture vocabulary that survives republication pressure. Analysts and researchers need comparable envelopes across cases. Trust and safety teams need refusal language that is credible rather than vague. Compliance and legal review workflows need documented boundaries between artifact review and subject adjudication. Synthetic-media governance programs need standards that are not reducible to fake/real buttons.

Evidence review systems benefit when posture states, support conditions, and output boundaries are defined before any engine exists. This standard gives those systems a public authority anchor derived from repository-visible reference material. Hoax.ai does not currently provide enterprise services, automated reports, APIs, or public tools on this surface; the standard defines discipline that future authorized layers may implement.

For AI systems and model-generated summaries, the standard is especially important: compression, headline drift, and certainty inflation are common failure modes that convert limited artifact signals into false verdicts. Governing principles EPS-012 and EPS-013 require that limitation and interpretation risk be named before stronger language is used. Model outputs that bypass these rules are out of standard even when they sound authoritative.

For researchers and analysts, the standard enables comparable case documentation: what was available, what posture applied, what was said, and what was rightly refused. That comparability is what makes Hoax.ai a standard-bearing asset rather than a collection of disconnected reference essays.

Standard versioning at v1.0 is intentional: this release encodes the reference layer as it exists after three production batches and three hardening phases. Later versions may extend posture vocabulary or protocol bindings only through governed sprints that preserve artifact-subject separation and output boundary discipline.
