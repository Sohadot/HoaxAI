Hoax.ai Category Language

Hoax.ai begins with language, not verdicts. Its public reference layer defines how evidence condition can be described before belief, action, publication, escalation, or institutional response.

Why Hoax.ai Starts With Language

Evidence-risk intelligence fails when teams reach for verdict language before they can describe evidence structure. In synthetic media conditions, appearance alone cannot carry trust. Institutions need a shared vocabulary for what can be said about an artifact’s condition before anyone treats it as sufficient grounds for confident action.

Hoax.ai treats category language as infrastructure, not marketing. Language names the system of judgment before any tool performs work. A public reader should understand how Hoax.ai speaks about evidence posture, artifact boundaries, source confidence, provenance gaps, output limits, and non-verdict reasoning without encountering upload surfaces, scoring interfaces, or fake-or-real outputs.

This page is the first governed public language layer. It makes the category legible by showing core terms, their relationships, and the boundaries that keep examination on artifacts rather than subjects. It does not operate an engine, classifier, or intake workflow.

The Core Question

Hoax.ai reframes the default question institutions ask when confronted with suspicious material. The rejected frame asks whether something is true. The adopted frame asks what condition the evidence is in.

That shift is not semantic decoration. It changes what teams may say, what they may infer, and what they may publish before further examination. Category language holds that shift in place so urgency does not collapse posture vocabulary back into binary certainty.

Readers who internalize this question can describe uncertainty without abandoning rigor. They can note when provenance is thin, when contextual fit is weak, or when coherence signals conflict—each as a structural observation rather than a leap toward conclusion about any connected subject.

Public Language Nodes

Eight language nodes structure Hoax.ai’s initial public vocabulary. Six are public reference anchors with dedicated reference pages. One remains a defined language node without an individual public route. One requires boundary refinement before any public route may be considered.

Artifact–Subject Separation

Public reference anchor

The rule that a statement about artifact condition must not become a statement about subject guilt, truthfulness, legitimacy, or conduct.

Source Confidence

Public reference anchor — Batch 1

How strongly source chain, custody signals, and publication path support bounded statements about artifact condition—not a source truth verdict.

Provenance Gap

Public reference anchor — Batch 1

Where origin, custody, or chain-of-record information is missing or weak—a gap does not prove manipulation or subject misconduct.

Not Assessable

Public reference anchor — Batch 1

A disciplined posture when available signals cannot support further structural description—not true, not false, not hidden judgment.

Evidence Chain

Context Collapse

Public reference anchor — Batch 2

Attribution Boundary

Public reference anchor — Batch 3

The limit that prevents artifact-level observation from becoming subject-level accusation—not guilt assignment or authorship certification.

The bounded support envelope of an artifact—what it can, partially can, and cannot support; limitation is credibility, not weakness.

How the Concepts Relate

Category language is not a flat glossary. Terms depend on one another. Evidence Posture sits at the center of the initial public model because it names artifact condition. That posture depends on Artifact–Subject Separation: without the boundary, posture language drifts toward subject accusation.

Evidence Posture is informed by Source Confidence and constrained by Provenance Gap. Where gaps dominate, posture descriptions must narrow. Not Assessable protects against false certainty when signals cannot support further structural description. Output Boundary limits what Hoax.ai may say even when internal analysis might suggest stronger language. Claim–Source Traceability governs support discipline for any statement that carries public weight. Synthetic Fragility names interpretive vulnerability in AI-era evidence. Evidence Chain and Context Collapse explain continuity and context loss that shape responsible reading. Attribution Boundary, Claim Drift, Evidence Limitation, and Interpretation Risk deepen constitutional discipline for attribution, circulation, envelope honesty, and overreading risk.

What This Language Prevents

Category language exists partly to block predictable failure modes. Without governed vocabulary, teams treat appearance as proof, urgency as permission, and artifact condition as proxy for subject judgment. Without output boundaries, posture labels become verdict substitutes. Without provenance discipline, confident language outruns available support.

This language layer prevents those collapses by keeping examination on artifacts, keeping statements bounded by available signals, and keeping non-verdict posture explicit when assessment cannot proceed responsibly. It does not replace forensic investigation, legal adjudication, or institutional review. It defines how Hoax.ai may speak before those processes complete.

Why There Is No Public Classifier Yet

Hoax.ai must teach how to speak about evidence posture before it offers any public classifier. A classifier without shared language would train readers to treat outputs as verdicts rather than bounded structural descriptions. Governance therefore blocks public engine, classifier, tool, upload, and scoring surfaces until category language is validated and future gates explicitly authorize operational layers.

Repository-visible governance documents record that block. This page educates; it does not classify, score, detect, or accept submissions. Future engine work remains blocked until governance allows it after language layer validation and subsequent approval.
