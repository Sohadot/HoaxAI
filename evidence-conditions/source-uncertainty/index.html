Condition definition

Source uncertainty is the evidence condition in which the apparent origin, publisher, witness, account, channel, or authority behind an artifact or claim cannot be clearly interpreted at review time.

Uncertainty may involve identity, role, timing, jurisdiction, or the relationship between a visible label and an inspectable chain. Source uncertainty is an evidence-condition term: it describes interpretive limits on source reading — not a moral accusation, not a manipulation finding, and not a binary authenticity label.

Within Hoax.ai, Source Uncertainty connects to related concepts and pathways as part of the evidence-condition library. The condition preserves artifact-first, non-accusatory posture language.

Conceptual thesis

Source uncertainty matters because synthetic circulation rewards plausible labels over inspectable origin. Readers who skip condition vocabulary often jump from unfamiliar publisher cues to verdict language.

Hoax.ai teaches reading the condition of source evidence before belief. The page thesis is stabilization: give institutions shared language that slows conclusion without pretending origin is always recoverable.
