Condition definition

Claim–evidence misalignment is the evidence condition in which an assertion, headline, caption, or institutional statement outruns the inspectable support available at review time.

Misalignment may involve headlines, captions, policy statements, or viral paraphrases that exceed inspectable artifact support. The condition names interpretive distance between assertion and support — not subject guilt, not deception findings, and not binary authenticity labels.

Within Hoax.ai, Claim–Evidence Misalignment connects to related concepts and pathways as part of the evidence-condition library. The condition preserves artifact-first, non-accusatory posture language.
